Your Business Collects Customer Data? Your PDPL Obligations

Does your business collect customer or employee data? Here are your actual obligations under Saudi Arabia's Personal Data Protection Law and how to comply.

The Personal Data Protection Law imposes specific obligations on any business processing personal data within the Kingdom, including obtaining explicit consent before processing, securing data against breaches, and immediate notification when any security incident occurs. Compliance is required for every business regardless of size. To review your business's status, reach out on WhatsApp.

Who Does the Law Cover?

The Personal Data Protection Law covers any entity processing personal data within the Kingdom, regardless of the business's size or activity, covering data collection, processing, storage, and transfer, imposing obligations on every business dealing with customer or employee data even if this processing is limited in scope.

Obtaining Explicit Consent Before Processing

We help businesses design clear mechanisms for obtaining individuals' explicit consent before processing their personal data, compliant with the law's requirements regarding this consent's form and clarity, since implicit or ambiguous consent may not be considered statutorily sufficient.

Internal Processing Policies for Businesses

We review personal data processing policies for technology and industrial businesses, confirming full compliance with the PDPL and its implementing regulations, including clearly defining processing purposes and appropriate data retention periods for each data type.

Securing Data Against Breaches

We help businesses understand the statutorily required technical requirements for securing sensitive data, including encrypting sensitive data and restricting access to authorized employees only, essential requirements to avoid any liability when a security incident occurs.

Response Procedures When a Security Breach Occurs

We help businesses design sound legal response procedures when a security breach occurs, including immediately documenting the incident and assessing statutory notification obligations to relevant authorities and affected parties within the defined statutory period, since delayed notification could be treated as a violation independent of the breach itself.

Transferring Data Outside the Kingdom

We help businesses understand statutory restrictions imposed on transferring personal data outside the Kingdom, and additional requirements to meet before any international transfer, especially for businesses dealing with cloud service providers or international partners.

How We Help

We review your business's current status regarding personal data collection and processing, and help you design policies and procedures fully compliant with the law before you're exposed to any violation that could cost substantial fines. Reach out on WhatsApp.

Frequently Asked Questions

Does the law cover small businesses too?

Yes, the law applies to every business processing personal data regardless of size, though larger companies' requirements may be more detailed depending on the volume of data processed.

What are the potential penalties for violating the law?

Penalties vary by the violation's nature and severity, and can reach substantial amounts in cases of serious breach of sensitive data protection obligations.

Does our website need a separate privacy policy?

Yes, having a clear privacy policy available to users explaining how their data is collected and processed is strongly recommended, and is a basic requirement for practical compliance with the law.
