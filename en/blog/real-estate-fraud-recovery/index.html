Victim of Real Estate Fraud? Immediate Recovery Steps

Victim of real estate fraud? Here are the immediate practical steps to recover your money, from initial documentation to the recovery lawsuit.

The first step upon discovering real estate fraud is immediately documenting all documents and correspondence with the fraudulent party, then filing a formal criminal report alongside a civil lawsuit to recover the funds paid. Acting quickly significantly increases the chances of recovering your right before the fraudster disposes of the funds. To help you, reach out on WhatsApp.

Common Forms of Real Estate Fraud

Real estate fraud takes multiple forms: selling a property the seller doesn't actually own, selling the same property to more than one buyer simultaneously, or promoting a fake, entirely unlicensed real estate project to collect reservation payments from buyers who will get nothing real in return.

Immediate Documentation Upon Discovering Fraud

As soon as you discover fraud, document every document related to the transaction: the signed contract, bank transfer receipts, text or WhatsApp correspondence with the fraudulent party, and any marketing advertisements your purchase decision relied on. This documentation is the foundation all subsequent proceedings are built on.

Immediate Criminal Reporting

We advise filing a formal criminal report as soon as fraud is discovered, as this report opens a formal investigation that could help freeze the fraudster's assets before they dispose of them or flee with the funds, an urgent action that shouldn't be delayed.

The Civil Recovery Lawsuit

Alongside the criminal report, we help you file a civil lawsuit to recover the amounts paid, relying on the signed contract and all documented evidence, and this civil path is separate from the criminal path and doesn't necessarily depend on its outcome.

Verifying Project Licensing Before Buying as Basic Prevention

The best protection from real estate fraud is prior prevention, by verifying any real estate project's licensing through the official Wafi platform before any financial payment, and confirming the seller's actual ownership of the property through a notarized title deed before signing any contract.

Cases of Mass Fraud Against Multiple Buyers

When it becomes clear that fraud affected multiple buyers of the same project or the same fraudster, coordinating among victims becomes useful to strengthen everyone's position before regulatory and judicial bodies, and we help organize this coordination effectively.

Frequently Asked Questions

How long does a real estate fraud recovery lawsuit take?

The timeline varies by the case's complexity and relevant bodies' cooperation, but acting quickly upon discovery significantly increases chances of actual recovery.

What if the fraudster has already moved the funds elsewhere?

You can still legally claim to recover your right, and this may include seizing any other assets the fraudster owns even if part of the funds were moved.

Can I act with only a civil lawsuit without a criminal report?

Yes, that's possible, but we always advise a criminal report in parallel since it opens investigation and asset-freezing tools not usually available in a civil lawsuit alone.
