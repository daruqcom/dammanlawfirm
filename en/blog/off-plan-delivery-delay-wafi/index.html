Your Unit Delivery Is Late — Your Wafi System Rights

Is your residential unit's delivery delayed beyond the agreed date? Here are your full rights under the Wafi system regulating off-plan sales.

The Wafi system grants you clear rights when your unit's delivery is delayed beyond the contract's agreed date, including claiming compensation for the delay period, and in cases of serious delay, the right to terminate the contract and recover the full amounts paid. The first step is verifying the project's status and license through the Wafi platform itself. To review your case, reach out on WhatsApp.

First, Verify the Project's Status Through the Wafi Platform

Before any other step, verify the project's license number and actual status through the official Wafi platform, as this verification clarifies whether the project's license remains valid, and what the actual announced completion percentage is compared to the original timeline agreed upon at purchase.

The Escrow Account and Protecting Your Paid Funds

The law requires developers to open an independent escrow account for each project, so buyers' funds aren't used except for that specific project. This means your funds are theoretically protected from being transferred to the developer's other projects, and if the project actually stalls, you can turn to this account's balance as one source for recovering paid funds.

Your Right to Compensation for the Delay Period

The off-plan sale contract and Wafi system provisions define your right to claim compensation for the delay period, and we help you precisely determine the amount owed based on your specific contract's clauses, since the compensation calculation formula differs from one developer to another based on what was agreed at signing.

When Do You Have the Right to Terminate the Contract Entirely?

When the delay is serious and unjustified — usually exceeding a relatively defined period per the contract, or accompanied by an actual halt in site work without convincing reason — you have the right to assess the option of terminating the contract entirely and claiming a full refund of paid amounts, rather than settling for partial compensation for the delay alone.

What If the Project Stalls Entirely?

In more serious cases where the project halts completely, the matter becomes more complex and may require coordinating with other buyers in the same project, or turning to relevant regulatory bodies monitoring the project's status, in addition to assessing your legal position toward the developer and the financing bank if one exists.

Documenting the Delay in a Way That Serves Your Position

We help you document every correspondence with the developer regarding the delay, dates of site visits if possible, and any formal notices you received regarding timeline adjustments, as this documentation forms the basis any subsequent claim is built on, whether through direct negotiation or legal escalation.

How We Help

Frequently Asked Questions

How long a delay justifies terminating the contract entirely?

There's no uniform period applying to all cases, and it depends on your specific contract's clauses and the delay's circumstances, so we review each case individually to assess your position's strength.

Does compensation include alternative rent during the delay period?

In some cases, additional compensation covering alternative housing costs can be claimed if the delay is long and unjustified, depending on each case's circumstances.

Can I sell my rights in the unit to another party while awaiting delivery?

This depends on your contract's clauses and Wafi system provisions regarding contract assignment, and we help you review this option if you're considering it.
