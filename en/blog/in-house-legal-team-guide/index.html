Is It Time to Hire In-House Legal Counsel?

Is it time to hire in-house counsel instead of relying solely on an external firm? Here are the signs actually indicating this need.

A business needs an in-house legal team once it reaches a certain scale of daily operations requiring frequent legal review, such as recurring supply contracts, periodic labor disputes, or ongoing government dealings — at that point an in-house team becomes more economically viable and faster to respond than permanently relying on an external firm. To help you assess your actual need, reach out on WhatsApp.

Not Every Business Needs an In-House Legal Team

Small and medium companies are often more financially efficient using an external firm as needed rather than hiring a permanent in-house team, especially if legal needs are intermittent and not recurring daily.

When Does an In-House Team Become More Viable?

Once a business reaches a certain scale of daily operations requiring frequent legal review — recurring supply contracts, periodic labor disputes, or ongoing government dealings — an in-house team becomes more economically viable and faster to respond, given its immediate availability without needing to schedule every inquiry with an external firm.

Designing the In-House Legal Team's Organizational Structure

We help businesses design their in-house legal team structure to fit their operations' size and complexity, from determining the number of counsel needed and their specializations, to defining reporting lines and authorities between the legal team and other executive departments.

Defining Internal Scope Versus External Engagement

One of the most important decisions any business faces when setting up its legal team is determining which matters are managed internally and which need a specialized external firm. We help clients establish a clear policy for this division, so routine matters like standard contracts are managed internally, while highly specialized cases or major disputes are referred to specialized external expertise.

The Actual Cost Compared to an External Firm

When comparing an in-house team's total cost (salaries, benefits, ongoing training) against an external firm's hourly or project fees, the comparison becomes clearer at a certain volume of recurring monthly inquiries, a tipping point that should be precisely assessed based on your actual data, not general assumptions.

A Hybrid Model: A Small In-House Team With Specialized External Support

Many businesses find a hybrid model most suitable: a small in-house team handling daily routine needs, while maintaining a relationship with a specialized external firm for major or specialized cases exceeding the in-house team's available expertise.

Frequently Asked Questions

How many monthly inquiries justify hiring in-house counsel?

There's no uniform number, but when legal consultations become a daily, regular part of your operations, this is a strong indicator of an in-house team's viability.

Can you start with just one in-house counsel?

Yes, many businesses start with one counsel covering basic needs, then gradually expand the team as actual need grows.

Does an in-house team fully eliminate the need for an external firm?

Rarely; even businesses with large in-house teams often maintain a relationship with an external firm for highly specialized cases or major disputes.
