Legal advisory for suppliers and contractors in the defense and aerospace sector.

A Defense and Aerospace Sector Growing With Military Industry Localization Under Vision 2030

The General Authority for Military Industries leads a national effort to localize a large share of defense spending within the Kingdom, opening major opportunities for local suppliers and contractors, but also imposing strict qualification and compliance requirements unlike any other commercial sector, given this sector's security sensitivity.

Supply and Manufacturing Contracts for Government and Defense Entities

We review and draft supply and manufacturing contracts for government and defense entities, including strict confidentiality clauses, special military quality requirements, and inspection and testing mechanisms before final delivery.

Qualification Requirements Before the General Authority for Military Industries

We help suppliers understand qualification requirements and regulatory compliance specific to this sensitive sector, including meeting local content percentage conditions and technology transfer requirements tied to the military industry localization program.

Subcontractor Disputes

We handle subcontractor disputes working with major companies in this field, particularly over delayed payments or disagreements interpreting precise technical specifications in military supply contracts.

Protecting Confidential Information and Defense Intellectual Property

We help companies operating in this sector draft strict confidentiality agreements with employees and partners, and protect the intellectual property of locally developed military technologies from leaks or unauthorized use.

Partnerships With Global Defense Manufacturing Companies

We help local companies wishing to partner with global military manufacturers structure these partnerships to achieve the technology transfer required by regulation, while protecting the local party's interests in the relationship.

Export Compliance With Defense Export Control Regulations

We help manufacturing companies understand international and local defense export control regulations, to avoid serious violations that could arise from exporting military technologies or products without required permits.

Security Clearance for Facilities and Personnel

We help companies operating in this sector understand security clearance requirements for their facilities and staff, an essential condition for handling classified information and contracts involving defense entities.

Military Aviation and the Emerging Space Sector

With the Kingdom's growing investment in the space and military aviation sector, we help startups in this field understand the regulatory frameworks specific to this emerging strategic sector.

Frequently Asked Questions

We want to apply as a supplier to the General Authority for Military Industries. What are the basic qualification requirements?

We help you review the qualification conditions specific to your activity, including local content requirements and required military quality standards.

The government body delayed paying our contractual dues. What are our options?

We review your contract to determine the available regulatory claim mechanism, and pursue collection through the appropriate channels for this type of government contract.

We are partnering with a global military manufacturer. How do we ensure the agreed technology transfer actually happens?

We draft the partnership agreement clearly defining technology transfer obligations, with compliance monitoring mechanisms throughout the partnership term.

A former employee leaked sensitive technical information to a competitor. What are our legal options?

We review the confidentiality agreement signed with them, and pursue a compensation claim for damages resulting from the breach of confidentiality, alongside any criminal report if warranted.

We are planning to export a locally developed defense product. Do we need a special permit?

Yes, exporting military technologies and products is subject to strict export control regulations, and we help you obtain the required permits before any shipment.

Can an unqualified foreign company participate in a defense tender through a local partner?

Yes, usually through a partnership structure meeting local content requirements, and we help you design this structure in compliance with the authority's requirements.
