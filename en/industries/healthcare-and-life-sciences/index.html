An Advanced Healthcare Sector Under Strict, Multi-Body Oversight

The healthcare sector in the Kingdom is subject to multi-layered oversight: the Ministry of Health for licensing, the Saudi Commission for Health Specialties for staff accreditation, and the Saudi Food and Drug Authority for medical products. This regulatory complexity means any healthcare facility needs legal coverage understanding all these layers together, not just one of them.

Legal Response to Malpractice Complaints

We help hospitals and clinics respond properly when a malpractice complaint is received, from immediate documentation of the medical file to coordinating with the competent medical error review committees, critical first steps that determine the file's entire trajectory.

Healthcare Licensing and Regulatory Compliance Requirements

We track healthcare licensing and regulatory compliance requirements before the Ministry of Health, including new healthcare facility licenses, renewing existing licenses, and responding to any violation notice threatening license continuity.

Contracts for Medical Research and Life Sciences Companies

We handle contracts for medical research and life sciences startups in the Eastern Province, including clinical trial agreements and required ethical approvals, and protecting intellectual property for research discoveries.

Employment Contracts for Foreign Medical Staff

Most major hospitals rely on foreign medical staff, and we help draft employment contracts accounting for Saudi Commission for Health Specialties qualification accreditation requirements and Saudi labor law end-of-service rights.

Privacy and Patient Health Data Protection

We help healthcare facilities comply with sensitive patient health data protection requirements, a data category subject to higher standards than ordinary personal data under the Personal Data Protection Law.

Mergers and Acquisitions Among Healthcare Facilities

We help investors and healthcare groups wishing to merge with or acquire smaller healthcare facilities understand Ministry of Health prior approval requirements, procedures requiring precise regulatory review of the existing licensing file.

Health Insurance and Medical Reimbursement

We help healthcare facilities understand their obligations toward health insurance companies, and represent them in disputes over denied claims or delayed payment, a recurring matter affecting private healthcare facilities' cash flow.

Frequently Asked Questions

We received a malpractice complaint from a patient. What are the correct first steps?

Preserve the complete medical file without alteration, and do not contact the patient directly before consulting us, since the first action often determines the strength of your position later.

We received a violation notice from the Ministry of Health threatening our license. How much time do we have to respond?

Deadlines are usually short and fixed, and we recommend immediate contact to assess the violation's basis and draft a response or grievance within the statutory period.

We are planning a new clinical trial. What ethical approvals are required?

We need approval from the hospital's or overseeing body's research ethics committee, and we help you prepare the approval file and coordinate with participants.

A foreign doctor's qualification accreditation has expired. Can they continue working?

Not without renewing accreditation with the Saudi Commission for Health Specialties, and we help you follow up this procedure to avoid an unlicensed operation violation.

Part of our patients' sensitive health data was leaked. What are our immediate obligations?

We help you assess the leak's scope and the notification required to the competent authorities and affected patients under the Personal Data Protection Law.

We are planning to acquire a small existing clinic. What is the most important clause to review?

Their Ministry of Health licensing status and any unresolved prior violations matter most, and we help you examine this file precisely before completing the deal.
