An Insurance Sector Under Strict SAMA Oversight and Rapidly Growing Demand

The insurance sector in the Kingdom is subject to close oversight from the Saudi Central Bank (SAMA), and as insurance awareness grows among individuals and businesses in the Eastern Province, the need for specialized legal coverage grows with it — one that understands the fundamental difference between an ordinary insurance dispute and one carrying a full regulatory dimension before the regulator. Businesses that engage this sector without specialized experience often find themselves in a weaker position when a major claim arises.

Reviewing Insurance Policies and Coverage Terms

We review insurance policies of every type, from health and vehicle insurance to major industrial property insurance, looking for any ambiguous exclusion clause the insurer might use to reject a future claim. This upfront review spares our clients the surprise of a coverage denial when an actual incident occurs.

Insurance Claims and Coverage Denials

When an insurance company rejects our client's claim without convincing justification, we act through an appeal before SAMA's insurance dispute resolution committee, a specialized body whose procedures differ from ordinary courts, requiring precise understanding of how it operates to ensure the appeal is accepted procedurally before being argued on the merits.

Insurance Broker Disputes With Their Companies

We also handle disputes between insurance brokers and the companies they represent, particularly over owed commissions and terminating brokerage agreements, a matter relevant to industrial businesses dealing with complex insurance policies through specialized brokers.

Takaful Insurance as a Sharia-Compliant Option

For clients preferring Takaful insurance, we help review Takaful policies and confirm their compliance with the Sharia controls SAMA imposes on this insurance type, including the surplus distribution mechanism among policyholders.

Mandatory Insurance for Industrial Facilities

Jubail and Dammam's industrial facilities are subject to mandatory insurance requirements for certain activities, and we help them understand the minimum required coverage and avoid penalties tied to non-compliance with these requirements.

Limitation Periods for Insurance Claims

Insurance claims are subject to defined limitation periods that can extinguish the right to claim if the insured delays filing, and we make sure to alert our clients to these deadlines from the very first moment an insured incident occurs, to avoid losing a legitimate right over a purely procedural delay.

Frequently Asked Questions

My insurance claim was denied over an ambiguous policy exclusion. What are my options?

We review the exclusion's wording precisely to determine whether it actually applies to your case, and if the denial is unjustified we appeal before SAMA's insurance dispute resolution committee.

How long does the insurance dispute committee take to rule on an appeal?

It varies with the file's complexity, but it is usually faster than ordinary litigation, and we follow the file with you step by step through to the decision.

My insurance broker did not pay my owed commission. Can they be sued?

Yes, we first review the brokerage agreement to confirm your entitlement, then proceed with a formal demand or a lawsuit if necessary.

Does Takaful insurance differ legally from conventional insurance when a dispute arises?

Yes, in terms of surplus distribution and Sharia compliance obligations, but the appeal path before SAMA is fundamentally similar, and we help you understand the finer distinctions.

Our industrial facility is unsure of the minimum required mandatory insurance. How do we verify it?

We review your activity and advise on the precise regulatory requirements applying to your sector, to avoid non-compliance penalties.

Can insurance policy terms be negotiated before signing?

Yes in many cases, especially for major industrial insurance policies, and we help you negotiate clearer terms before committing to them.
