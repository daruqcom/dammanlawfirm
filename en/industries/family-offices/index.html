Family Offices Managing Growing Wealth Amid Generational Transition Challenges

Family offices in the Eastern Province manage diverse assets spanning real estate to shares in family businesses and financial investments, and today face a shared challenge: how this wealth transitions smoothly to the next generation without turning into a dispute that breaks apart the family and the wealth together.

Estate Planning and Asset Distribution

We help family offices with estate planning and asset distribution that preserves family wealth stability across generations, through wills and ownership structures designed to avoid fragmentation upon the wealth founder's death, while respecting mandatory Sharia inheritance rules that cannot be bypassed.

Family Governance Structures

We review and design family governance structures, including family charters governing decision-making, the next generation's entry into management, and resolving disputes between different family branches before they escalate into an open conflict.

Partner Agreements Among Family Members

When family assets are managed through a holding company owned by several individuals, we help draft partner agreements clarifying each party's rights and the exit or sale mechanism, terms often neglected in the early stages and later becoming a source of dispute.

Structuring Family Investments

We help family offices structure their local and international investments with legal and tax efficiency, accounting for the regulatory differences between direct investment and investment through intermediary entities.

Resolving Family Disputes Over Wealth

When a disagreement arises between family members over managing or distributing the wealth, we step in as a neutral party helping reach a resolution that preserves family relationships as much as possible, resorting to litigation only when an amicable resolution proves impossible.

Family Assets Outside the Kingdom

Many families own assets in the Gulf, Europe, or the Americas, and we help them coordinate cross-border family planning, working with correspondent firms in those countries to ensure the legal structure is consistent everywhere.

Frequently Asked Questions

The wealth founder refuses to discuss succession planning. How do we start the conversation?

We help families frame this conversation as protecting the wealth, not as a signal of retirement or death, and having a neutral party like a legal advisor present is often helpful in opening this discussion.

We have a family holding company and fear a dispute among second-generation members. What is the solution?

We design a family charter and partner agreement governing each branch's authorities, the decision-making mechanism, and the exit valuation for anyone wishing to leave, before conflict imposes a worse solution.

We own property in more than one country. Do we need separate planning for each?

Yes from a regulatory standpoint, but we coordinate this planning through correspondent firms to ensure the family's overall strategy stays consistent despite differing local regulations.

One family member manages the wealth in a way others see as non-transparent. What are our options?

We help you establish governance mechanisms ensuring transparency, such as periodic reports and a family board of trustees, without needing to escalate the matter to an open dispute.

Can an old estate plan be updated after the wealth's size or family composition changes?

Yes, and we recommend periodically reviewing the estate plan every few years or upon any material change in assets or family members.

We want to involve the third generation in managing family wealth. How do we start this transition safely?

We recommend a gradual transition through advisory roles or representation on the family board before handing over full management authority, to ensure their readiness and protect the wealth's stability.
