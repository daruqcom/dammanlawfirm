Domestic Worker Disputes

Domestic Worker Disputes Lawyers in Dammam

Resolving domestic-worker contract disputes between families and domestic staff under the Domestic Workers Law, from contract termination to absconding cases.

Domestic Worker Consultation on WhatsApp
Saudi Arabia's Domestic Workers Law governs the relationship between a family and a domestic worker under a contract entirely separate from the general Labor Law, with its own dispute-resolution mechanism through the Musaned platform and the Ministry of Human Resources. This independent system means many concepts common in ordinary employment contracts don't necessarily apply to this relationship. The next step is reaching out to review the contract and complaint details.

The Difference Between the Domestic Workers System and the General Labor Law

We clarify to families that a domestic worker contract is subject to a system entirely independent of the general Labor Law, with special provisions on rest hours, days off, and dispute-resolution mechanism, fundamentally different from what applies to ordinary employment contracts.

Ending a Domestic Worker Contract

We help families and recruitment offices settle disputes over ending a domestic-worker contract, whether the family initiates termination or the domestic worker wishes to end the relationship, clarifying each party's due rights.

Unpaid Wage Claims

We help domestic workers claim unpaid wages through the Musaned platform, and also help families understand their correct obligations toward paying wages on their set schedule.

Reported and Unreported Absconding Cases

We clarify to families the correct procedure for reporting a domestic worker's absconding through the Musaned platform, a necessary procedure to avoid legal responsibility for the worker's continued stay in the country without permission.

The Domestic Worker's Right to a Safe Work Environment

We clarify to families their obligations to provide a safe, decent work and living environment for the domestic worker, fundamental obligations whose breach entails legal responsibility that could affect future recruitment.

Working Hours and Rest Day Disputes

We help parties resolve disputes over daily working hours and weekly rest days due to the domestic worker under the Domestic Workers Law, a recurring point of disagreement between the parties.

Recruitment Agency Liability Toward the Contract

We help families understand the recruitment agency's liability for ensuring the worker matches agreed specifications, coordinating with our recruitment agency dispute services for comprehensive coverage of this aspect.

Renewal and Termination at the Contract Term's End

We clarify to families their options as the domestic worker contract's term approaches its end, whether renewal or termination, and statutory procedures to follow in each case.

Health Insurance and Medical Care for the Domestic Worker

We clarify to families their obligation to provide valid health insurance for the domestic worker throughout the contract's term, a mandatory obligation often overlooked at annual renewal, even though a lapse can expose the family to direct liability for any treatment costs arising during the gap.

Transferring a Domestic Worker's Services Between Families

We help families wishing to transfer a domestic worker's services from another family understand the statutory procedures for this transfer, including both parties' consent and settling any pending financial dues before completing the transfer.

Frequently Asked Questions

Where is a domestic-worker dispute complaint filed?

Usually through the Musaned platform run by the Ministry of Human Resources and Social Development.

What if the domestic worker leaves without permission?

The absence must be officially reported through the relevant platform to avoid legal responsibility for their continued stay in the country.

Our domestic worker is claiming unpaid wages. What's the correct procedure?

We help you review your payment record and confirm your full compliance, or help the worker claim their right if the delay is actual.

We want to end our domestic worker's contract before its term ends. What are our obligations?

We clarify the worker's due rights upon this early termination, and statutory procedures to follow.

Is there a specific number of daily rest hours a domestic worker is entitled to?

Yes, the Domestic Workers Law defines these hours, and we clarify the precise details to avoid any violation.

The worker we recruited doesn't match specifications agreed with the recruitment agency. What are our options?

We help you claim their replacement or a fee refund per guarantee terms with the recruitment agency.

We forgot to renew our domestic worker's health insurance. Does this expose us to liability?

Yes, a lapse could make you directly liable for any treatment costs arising during the gap, and we recommend addressing this immediately.
