Weapons Offenses

Weapons Offenses Lawyers in Dammam

Weapons offenses include unlicensed possession, carrying, or trafficking of firearms or bladed weapons, with penalties differing fundamentally by weapon type, case circumstances, and the accused's intent.

Weapons cases in the Saudi system cover a wide range: from possessing an unlicensed firearm, to carrying it in a public place, up to a more serious charge like illegal weapons trafficking. The penalty varies significantly based on weapon type, seizure circumstances, and the accused's actual intent. Types of Weapons Charges and Their Varying Severity

A weapons charge's severity differs fundamentally by type: possessing a firearm originally licensed but with an unrenewed license differs entirely from carrying an entirely unlicensed weapon in a public place, and both differ from an illegal weapons trafficking charge carrying far harsher penalties. Understanding your charge's precise classification is the first step in building an appropriate defense strategy.

Reviewing the Legality of Seizure and Search Procedures

We always scrutinize the legality of the seizure and search procedures that led to discovering the weapon; any search conducted without a valid judicial warrant or reasonable suspicion cause could invalidate the evidence extracted from it entirely, a fundamental defense in many weapons cases.

Defending Possession of a Licensed Weapon With an Expired License

A common situation is possessing a weapon originally licensed but whose license expired without renewal, a violation far lighter than possessing an entirely unlicensed weapon. We help the accused clarify this fundamental distinction before the court to mitigate the classification and potential penalty.

Defending Carrying a Weapon in a Public Place

Carrying a weapon, even a licensed one, in certain places or occasions could constitute a separate violation from mere possession. We review each incident's circumstances to determine whether this charge is justified given the actual circumstances or subject to challenge.

Illegal Weapons Trafficking Cases

The illegal weapons trafficking charge is among this file's most serious, carrying severe penalties. We defend the accused in these cases through precise review of presented evidence, especially evidence of criminal intent the prosecution must clearly establish, not mere suspicion.

Firearms Versus Bladed Weapons

Regulations applying to firearms differ fundamentally from those applying to bladed weapons, and we help the accused understand their weapon's precise classification and the regulations specifically relevant to it, since each category is subject to different penalties and procedures.

Weapon Possession for Self-Defense or Property Protection

Some accused claim weapon possession was for self-defense or property protection purposes, and this defense may be acceptable in certain circumstances but needs precise proof of an actual serious threat, and we help build this defense convincingly before the court.

Long-Term Effects of a Weapons Case Conviction

A weapons case conviction can carry effects beyond the direct penalty, such as difficulty obtaining certain licenses in the future or affecting employment prospects in some sectors. We clarify these long-term effects to clients within our comprehensive defense strategy.

Weapons Cases for Non-Saudi Residents

Weapons cases carry additional dimensions for non-Saudi residents, as a conviction could accompany deportation procedures or future entry bans, alongside the direct criminal penalty. We help non-Saudi accused understand this additional dimension and build a defense accounting for it.

Confiscation of Seized Weapons and Recovery Procedures

When a weapon is seized as part of a certain case, questions arise about its final fate after proceedings conclude, especially if the accused's innocence is established or the charge mitigated to a lighter violation. We help clients follow up procedures to recover the seized weapon if recovery is regulatorily possible.

Frequently Asked Questions

My car was searched and a weapon discovered without a judicial warrant. Can this procedure be challenged?

Yes, we review the search procedures' legality, and any search conducted without sufficient legal cause could invalidate the evidence extracted from it entirely.

My weapon was licensed but I forgot to renew the license. Is this considered the same violation as possessing an entirely unlicensed weapon?

No, this is a far lighter violation, and we help you clarify this fundamental distinction before the court to mitigate the classification and penalty.

I was charged with weapons trafficking despite owning only one piece. Is this classification correct?

We review the actual evidence of trafficking intent, since merely owning one weapon may not suffice to establish this more serious charge, and we help you challenge this classification if unjustified.

Can weapon possession be defended on grounds of self-defense?

Yes in certain circumstances, but it needs precise proof of an actual serious threat, and we help you build this defense with convincing evidence.

What's the penalty difference between possessing a firearm and possessing a bladed weapon?

The applicable regulations differ fundamentally between the two categories, and we clarify your weapon's precise classification and the potential penalties specifically tied to it.

Does a weapons case conviction affect my future employment prospects?

It may affect certain sectors or when applying for certain licenses in the future, and we clarify these long-term effects within our comprehensive defense strategy.
