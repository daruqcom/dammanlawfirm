Alcohol & Intoxicants Offenses

Alcohol & Intoxicants Offenses Lawyers in Dammam

Alcohol and intoxicants offenses include manufacturing, possession, dealing, or consuming intoxicants, subject to relatively strict penalties under Saudi law requiring precise defense from the moment of initial seizure.

Alcohol and intoxicants cases cover a wide range of criminalized acts: from simple personal use possession to manufacturing or dealing in commercial quantities, with fundamentally varying penalties based on the act's type, seized quantity, and case circumstances. Any seizure procedure needs precise review of its legality from the first moment. Classifying Alcohol Cases by the Act's Nature

An alcohol charge's severity differs fundamentally by the act's nature: simple personal consumption differs entirely from possession in quantities indicating dealing intent, and both differ from a commercial manufacturing or dealing charge carrying far harsher penalties. Understanding your charge's precise classification is essential to building an appropriate defense.

Reviewing the Legality of Seizure and Search Procedures

We always scrutinize the legality of the seizure and search procedures that led to discovering the intoxicants; any search conducted without a valid judicial warrant or reasonable suspicion cause could invalidate the evidence extracted from it entirely, a fundamental defense in many of these cases.

Defending Simple Personal Possession Cases

We help the accused in possessing simple quantities indicating personal use clarify this fundamental distinction from a dealing charge, which carries a far harsher penalty, and we build the defense based on the actual seized quantity and seizure circumstances.

Commercial Manufacturing and Dealing Cases

The commercial manufacturing or dealing charge for intoxicants is among this file's most serious, carrying severe penalties. We defend the accused in these cases through precise review of presented evidence, especially evidence of commercial intent the prosecution must clearly establish.

The Right to Legal Counsel From the First Interrogation Moment

We always advise against making any statement or confession before consulting a lawyer, since any unconsidered statement during initial interrogation could later be used against the accused before the court, even if the actual legal position was stronger than it appeared at that moment.

Medical Examinations and Their Results as Evidence

When the case involves a medical examination to prove consumption, we review the precision of the medical procedures followed and the sample chain of custody, since any flaw in these procedures could form a basis for challenging the examination result presented as evidence.

Alcohol Cases for Non-Saudi Residents

Alcohol cases carry additional dimensions for non-Saudi residents, as a conviction could accompany deportation procedures or future entry bans, alongside the direct criminal penalty. We help non-Saudi accused understand this additional dimension.

Seeking Penalty Mitigation and Mitigating Circumstances

We help the accused highlight any mitigating circumstances that could affect penalty assessment, such as no prior criminal record or certain personal circumstances, within a comprehensive defense strategy aiming for the best possible outcome.

Distinguishing Personal Possession From Possession With Intent to Deal

The difference between possessing a small quantity for personal use and a larger quantity with intent to deal is fundamental in determining the penalty, and we help the accused build a defense clarifying this difference based on the actual quantity and precise seizure circumstances.

Frequently Asked Questions

My car was searched and intoxicants discovered without a judicial warrant. Can this procedure be challenged?

Yes, we review the search procedures' legality, and any search conducted without sufficient legal cause could invalidate the evidence extracted from it entirely.

I was charged with dealing despite the seized quantity being small. Is this classification correct?

We review actual evidence of dealing intent, since a small quantity alone may not suffice to establish this more serious charge, and we help you challenge this classification if unjustified.

I made a confession during initial interrogation before consulting a lawyer. Does this harm my position?

It may affect your position, but we review this confession's circumstances and the legality of surrounding procedures to assess the possibility of challenging it.

The medical examination I underwent showed a result I doubt is accurate. Can it be challenged?

Yes, we review the sample's chain of custody and the precision of medical procedures followed, and any flaw in them could form a strong basis for challenging the result.

I'm a non-Saudi resident accused in an alcohol case. Do conviction consequences differ for me?

Yes, conviction could accompany deportation procedures or future entry bans, and we clarify this additional dimension within our defense strategy.

I have no prior criminal record. Does this affect the potential penalty?

Yes, we help you highlight this mitigating circumstance and any other personal circumstances that could positively affect penalty assessment.

What's the difference between simple possession for personal use and possession with intent to deal?

The difference is fundamental in determining the penalty, and we help you build a defense clarifying this difference based on the actual seized quantity and precise circumstances.
