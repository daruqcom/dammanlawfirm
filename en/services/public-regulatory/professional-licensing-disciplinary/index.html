Professional Licensing & Disciplinary

Professional Licensing & Disciplinary Lawyers in Dammam

Professional licenses are required to practice a large number of professions in the Kingdom, from medicine to engineering and accounting, and any regulatory violation may expose the license holder to disciplinary penalties up to license revocation.

Professional Licensing Consultation on WhatsApp
Professional licenses are the statutory requirement to practice certain professions subject to specialized professional bodies' oversight, such as the Saudi Commission for Health Specialties for physicians, or the Saudi Council of Engineers. Any violation of professional practice standards may be referred to a competent disciplinary committee, with penalties ranging from a warning to final license revocation. The next step is reaching out on WhatsApp to review your professional situation or the violation you're facing.

Professions Subject to Mandatory Professional Licensing

A large number of professions in the Kingdom require mandatory professional licensing before practice: physicians and health professionals, engineers, accountants, and lawyers, among other professions. Each profession is subject to a specialized regulatory body defining licensing standards and required ethical practice.

Meeting Initial Licensing Requirements

We help new professionals understand and meet initial licensing requirements, from required academic qualifications to necessary training periods or practical experience, and follow application procedures before the competent professional body.

Renewing Professional Licenses

Most professional licenses need periodic renewal, and this renewal may require meeting continuing training hours or professional development, and we help professionals follow these requirements to avoid unintentional license expiry.

Referral to Professional Disciplinary Committees

When a complaint against a certain professional practice reaches a disciplinary committee, we defend the accused professional through precise review of the complaint and presented evidence, building a defense clarifying their actual compliance with professional practice standards.

Common Ethical and Professional Violations

Common violations include breaching professional confidentiality standards, undisclosed conflicts of interest, or negligence in delivering professional service at the expected quality level. We help professionals understand these standards and avoid unintentional violations.

Disciplinary Violation Penalties and Their Degrees

Professional violation penalties range from a simple warning to temporary practice suspension, up to final license revocation in serious violations. We help professionals understand their violation's expected degree and build a defense mitigating this penalty as much as possible.

Appealing Disciplinary Committee Decisions

When a disciplinary committee's decision is unfair or based on a procedural defect, we help the professional appeal this decision before the higher body, relying on precise review of the procedures' soundness followed at the first stage.

Protecting the Professional's Reputation During Investigation

We always advise professionals to handle any complaint or investigation cautiously before consulting a lawyer, since any unconsidered statement or response could later be used against them before the disciplinary committee, and we help them manage this sensitive stage wisely.

Rehabilitation and Re-Licensing After a Suspension Period

After a temporary practice suspension period ends, we help professionals meet any additional requirements to reactivate their license, such as proving the original violation's causes were addressed or completing compensatory training hours.

The Difference Between Disciplinary Accountability and Civil or Criminal Accountability

We clarify to professionals that disciplinary accountability before the professional committee is a path separate from any civil or criminal accountability that might arise from the same incident, and a professional could face both paths simultaneously depending on the violation's nature.

Frequently Asked Questions

I received a professional complaint against me referred to a disciplinary committee. What's the first step I should take?

Don't respond to the complaint without consulting a lawyer first; any unconsidered statement could later be used against you, and contact us immediately to build an appropriate defense.

My professional license expired without renewal because I forgot the date. What are my options?

We help you understand available reactivation procedures and any additional requirements that might be imposed due to this delay.

Can a decision to revoke my professional license be appealed?

Yes if there's a procedural or substantive defect in the decision, and we help you assess this appeal's chances before the competent higher body.

A colleague in my profession accuses me of an undisclosed conflict of interest. How do I defend myself?

We review the accusation's details and the alleged relationship's nature, and help you clarify your actual position before the disciplinary committee with supporting evidence.

What's the difference between a simple warning and temporary suspension in disciplinary penalties?

A warning doesn't affect your continued professional practice, while temporary suspension prevents you from practicing for a defined period, and we clarify your violation's expected penalty specifically.

Are continuing training hours mandatory for renewing all professional licenses?

It varies by profession and overseeing body, and we help you understand your profession's precise requirements before the renewal date.

Could I face a civil or criminal lawsuit alongside disciplinary accountability for the same violation?

Yes, these are two separate paths that could combine depending on the violation's nature, and we help you manage both in a coordinated way.
