Marriage Dissolution & Paternity

Marriage Dissolution & Paternity Lawyers in Dammam

Marriage dissolution is a judicial procedure to end a marriage for specific reasons such as harm, non-support, or long absence, and paternity confirmation is a sensitive legal matter requiring precise evidence and full legal documentation.

Marriage Dissolution Consultation on WhatsApp
Marriage dissolution is a judicial path different from ordinary divorce, resorted to by the wife when the husband refuses divorce despite actual harm befalling her, such as non-support, long absence, or repeated harm. Paternity confirmation is a separate matter concerning determining a child's legal father, whether confirming or denying it, needing precise legal evidence given its deep effect on the child's identity and future. Statutorily Accepted Grounds for Requesting Marriage Dissolution

The law accepts marriage dissolution requests for specific reasons: harm befalling the wife from the husband's treatment, non-support with his inability or refusal established, long absence without acceptable excuse, or a defect preventing normal continuation of married life.

Filing a Marriage Dissolution Suit Procedures

We help wives file a marriage dissolution suit before the personal status court, starting from documenting the claimed harm or reason with necessary evidence, through to pleading establishing entitlement to dissolution before the judge.

Proving Harm as Grounds for Marriage Dissolution

We help wives document the harm they've suffered, whether physical, psychological, or financial, through witness testimony and medical reports if necessary, since this documentation is essential to the dissolution suit's success.

Marriage Dissolution for Non-Support

When the husband refuses to provide support despite his ability, or his actual inability to support is established, we help the wife file a dissolution suit for this reason, documenting the refusal period and previous alimony claim attempts.

Establishing Paternity and Its Legal Evidence

We help in paternity confirmation cases, whether through recognized Sharia presumptions or modern evidence like genetic testing when necessary and statutorily accepted, to conclusively establish a child's paternity to their legal father.

Denying Paternity and Its Strict Controls

Denying paternity carries extremely strict Sharia and statutory controls, and isn't easily accepted given its serious effect on the child, and we help parties precisely understand these controls before considering this sensitive path.

Financial and Custody Implications of Marriage Dissolution

We clarify to parties the financial and custody implications arising from marriage dissolution, which may differ from ordinary divorce implications based on the dissolution's specific reason and each case's circumstances.

Protecting Case Privacy Throughout Proceedings

We recognize these cases' utmost sensitivity, especially paternity confirmation or denial, and ensure maximum confidentiality throughout proceedings, from initial contact with us through the final judgment's issuance.

Marriage Dissolution for Physical or Psychological Harm

We help wives experiencing actual physical or psychological harm document this harm with statutorily accepted evidence, and file a marriage dissolution suit protecting their safety without needing the husband's consent to divorce.

Yes, long absence without excuse is among statutorily accepted grounds for dissolution, and we help you document the absence duration and circumstances.
