Custody & Alimony

Custody & Alimony Lawyers in Dammam

Custody determines who cares for the children after divorce based on the child's best interest standard, and alimony is the wife's and children's ongoing financial right, both among the most sensitive and future-affecting personal status matters.

Custody & Alimony Consultation on WhatsApp
Custody and alimony are among the most recurring and sensitive personal status matters: custody determines which parent handles daily childcare after divorce based on the child's best interest standard, and alimony is the ongoing financial obligation toward the wife and children. Both matters need handling balancing legal precision with the deep human sensitivity of these disputes. The next step is reaching out in complete confidence on WhatsApp.

The Child's Best Interest Standard in Determining Custody

The court determines custody based on one fundamental standard: the child's best interest, not either parent's wish independent of this standard. We help parties understand the factors actually affecting this assessment, such as the child's environment stability and capacity to provide suitable care.

Priority Order for Custodians Under Sharia Rulings

Islamic jurisprudence defines a specific priority order for custodians, usually starting with the mother then moving to other relatives in a defined order if the mother's custody isn't possible for some reason. We help parties understand this order and how it applies to their specific circumstances.

Non-Custodial Parent's Visitation and Communication Rights

We represent the non-custodial parent in securing their right to regularly visit and communicate with their children, a fundamental right that doesn't lapse merely because they didn't obtain custody, and help draft a practical visitation arrangement considering the child's interest and stability.

Custody Revocation and Its Statutory Grounds

Specific statutory grounds exist that could lead to revoking a parent's custody, such as establishing serious negligence or circumstances harming the child's interest. We help parties understand these grounds and realistically assess their position, whether seeking custody revocation or defending against this pursuit.

Determining a Fair Alimony Amount

We help fairly assess due alimony accounting for the husband's actual income and the wife's and children's real needs, not arbitrary estimated amounts that could be excessive or insufficient.

Collecting Overdue Alimony

When the husband delays paying due alimony, we help the wife claim full arrears through appropriate judicial procedures, including mandatory enforcement requests if refusal to pay continues.

Adjusting the Alimony Amount When Circumstances Change

When either party's financial circumstances change fundamentally, such as the husband losing his job or children's needs increasing as they grow older, we help submit an alimony adjustment request reflecting this actual change.

The Custodian Relocating to Another City

When the custodian wishes to relocate to another city with the children, potential tension arises with the other parent's regular visitation right. We help parties reach an arrangement balancing the custodian's freedom to choose their residence with the other party's right to communicate with their children.

Protecting Children From Being Affected by Custody and Alimony Disputes

We always advise keeping children entirely isolated from the details of any dispute over their custody or alimony, since these disputes should stay between the adult parents without burdening the child with unnecessary additional psychological weight.

Frequently Asked Questions

My ex-husband refuses to let me see my children despite a custody ruling in my favor. What are my options?

We help you file a mandatory enforcement request for the custody ruling, with the possibility of taking additional action if refusal to enable access continues.

My husband stopped paying alimony months ago. How do I claim the arrears?

We help you file a lawsuit claiming full arrears, with a mandatory enforcement request if necessary to ensure actual collection.

I lost my job and can no longer pay alimony at the previously set amount. What do I do?

We help you submit an alimony adjustment request reflecting your actual changed financial circumstances, instead of continuing an obligation you cannot fulfill.

I want to relocate to another city with my children after obtaining custody. Is this an absolute right?

We help you assess this right balanced against the other party's right to communicate with their children, reaching a practical arrangement considering everyone's interest.

Can a mother's custody be revoked if she remarries another man?

It depends on the case's details and its actual effect on the child's interest, and we help you precisely assess your position based on your specific circumstances.

How is a fair alimony amount calculated for my children?

We review the husband's actual income and children's real needs to reach a fair assessment, away from arbitrary estimated amounts that could be unrealistic.

How do I protect my children from being psychologically affected by the ongoing custody or alimony dispute between me and my ex-husband?

We advise keeping children entirely isolated from this dispute's details, and help you manage legal procedures without burdening them with any additional weight.
