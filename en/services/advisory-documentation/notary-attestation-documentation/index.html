Notary, Attestation & Documentation

Notary & Attestation & Documentation Lawyers in Dammam

Notarizing documents before a notary public gives them official evidentiary force before courts and government bodies, a necessary step, not a luxury, when dealing with important contracts and powers of attorney.

Notary and attestation services give your documents the official force needed to be relied upon before courts and government bodies, whether a power of attorney, a contract, or a declaration. In the Kingdom, most notarization procedures are now done electronically via the Najiz platform, but some cases still require physical attendance or legal intervention to avoid application rejection. Why Does a Document Need Official Notarization at All?

An unnotarized contract or declaration may be valid between its parties, but it loses its full evidentiary force before the judiciary if one party later denies it. Notarization before a notary public transforms the document from a mere signed paper into an official instrument difficult to challenge, a difference that becomes clear at the moment of dispute, when one party needs to prove a certain legal act occurred before the court.

The Najiz Platform and Electronic Notarization

The Ministry of Justice has moved most notarization procedures to the electronic Najiz platform, including notarizing powers of attorney, declarations, and some simple contracts, without needing physical attendance in many cases. We help clients use this platform correctly, especially when a power of attorney includes complex authorities requiring precise drafting to avoid later application rejection.

Notarizing Contracts and Powers of Attorney for Non-Residents

When one party to a document resides outside the Kingdom, notarization becomes more complex, since the document usually needs attestation through the Saudi embassy or consulate in the country of residence, then final local attestation. We follow up these procedures on our clients' behalf from start until the document becomes fully effective, a process that can take longer than simple local notarization.

Special Powers of Attorney and Precise Authority Drafting

A loose general power of attorney may be rejected by some government or banking bodies for lack of clarity in the specific authority required, and we help clients draft special powers of attorney defining authorities precisely enough for the intended purpose without granting excess authority that could be exploited. This balance between clarity and protection is what distinguishes a carefully drafted power of attorney from a generic ready-made template.

Notarizing Major Commercial Contracts

Some commercial contracts, especially those tied to property ownership transfer, need additional official notarization to ensure full effect, and we help commercial parties understand when notarization is mandatory by law and when it is optional but advisable to strengthen the evidentiary position later.

Official Declarations and Their Legal Effect

A notarized declaration, whether acknowledging a debt or waiving a right, carries evidentiary force that makes retracting it later extremely difficult. We help clients understand the full effect of any declaration before signing and notarizing it, avoiding unintended obligations arising from imprecise drafting.

Common Reasons for Notarization Request Rejection

A notarization request is often rejected for simple formal reasons: a missing supporting document, ambiguity in authority wording, or data mismatch with official records. We review your request before submission to avoid these rejections that cost valuable time, especially when notarization is tied to a tight deadline for a deal or another procedure.

Notarization for Litigation Purposes

When a document is needed as evidence in a pending or anticipated lawsuit, we help notarize it in a way that achieves the maximum possible evidentiary force before the court, accounting for any special procedural requirements the nature of the case imposes.

Notarizing Marriage Contracts and Related Financial Declarations

Some marriage contracts include special financial terms or property declarations, and we help parties notarize these terms with legal precision that protects both parties' rights in the future, especially in marriages involving significant commercial or real estate assets needing clear ownership clarification from the start.

Digital Notarization Through Mobile and the Absher Application

Some simple notarization procedures have become available directly through the Absher application, without even needing the separate Najiz platform, and we help clients understand which procedures can be completed through this faster path, and which still need the longer path through a traditional notary public.

Retaining and Retrieving Notarized Document Copies Later

We always advise clients to retain digital and paper copies of every notarized document, since some bodies request the original or a recently attested copy even if the document was notarized years earlier, and we help obtain official replacement copies when the original is lost or damaged.

Notarizing Corporate Documents and General Assembly Minutes

Companies frequently need to notarize general assembly meeting minutes and board resolutions, especially when amending the founding contract, increasing capital, or changing partners. We help businesses prepare and notarize these minutes in a way that meets Ministry of Commerce requirements and other regulators relevant to their activity.

Cost and Expected Time for Each Type of Notarization Procedure

Notarization cost and duration vary with document type and complexity; notarizing a simple power of attorney through Najiz might take minutes, while notarizing a major commercial contract requiring prior legal review could take days. We clarify to clients from the start the expected cost and timeline for their specific case, without later surprises.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can a power of attorney be notarized for someone residing outside Saudi Arabia?

Yes, through the Saudi embassy or consulate in their country of residence, then local attestation, and we follow up these procedures on your behalf.

Why was my notarization request rejected on the Najiz platform?

Usually for a formal reason like a missing document or ambiguous authority, and we review your request to determine the cause and correct it before resubmitting.

Is a general power of attorney sufficient, or do I need a special one?

It depends on the purpose; government and banking bodies often prefer a special power of attorney with defined authorities, and we help you draft it precisely.

Can a notarized declaration be retracted later?

Retraction is very difficult after notarization, so we recommend reviewing the declaration and its full legal effect with us before signing and notarizing.

How long does notarizing a major commercial contract involving ownership transfer take?

It varies with the contract type and how complete the supporting documents are, and we help speed up the procedure by preparing a complete file from the start.

Do I need a lawyer just to notarize a simple document via Najiz?

Not always, but when the power of attorney or contract involves complex authorities or parties residing abroad, prior legal review saves you from application rejection or a later dispute.
