Due Diligence & Business Intelligence

Due Diligence & Business Intelligence Lawyers in Dammam

Legal due diligence uncovers hidden risks before completing a major investment deal, business partnership, or acquisition, while correcting course is still possible.

Due Diligence Consultation on WhatsApp
Legal due diligence is an organized examination of the other party in a potential deal, uncovering hidden legal, financial, or regulatory risks before signing any final commitment. Whether you are acquiring a company, entering a business partnership, or investing in a new project, upfront review is far cheaper than discovering a problem after the deal closes. The next step is reaching out on WhatsApp describing the deal you are planning.

Why a Surface-Level Review Isn't Enough Before a Major Deal

Many investors settle for reviewing financial statements and the proposed contract, overlooking that the most dangerous risks often lie in details that don't appear in core documents: a pending lawsuit, an unresolved regulatory violation, or a hidden contractual obligation with a third party. Organized due diligence uncovers these risks before they become your liability after signing the deal.

Regulatory Review of Licenses and Compliance

We review the other party's licensing status and regulatory compliance record, including any unresolved prior violations that could transfer to you as liability after acquisition or partnership. This review is especially critical in heavily regulated sectors like finance, healthcare, and defense industries.

Reviewing Existing Contracts and Contractual Obligations

We examine the other party's existing contracts with its customers, employees, and suppliers, looking for clauses that could form an unexpected burden after the deal, such as long-term exclusivity clauses or deferred financial obligations not clearly disclosed during initial negotiations.

Pending and Potential Litigation

We verify whether any pending or threatened lawsuits exist against the other party, whether as defendant or plaintiff, because the outcome of any ongoing dispute could materially affect the deal's value or even the continuity of operations after completion.

Reviewing Intellectual Property and Intangible Assets

When the deal involves significant value in intellectual property such as trademarks, patents, or source code, we verify these rights are properly registered and free of any ownership dispute that could reduce their actual value after the deal.

Ownership Structure and Internal Governance

We review the target company's actual ownership structure, including any hidden shareholder agreements or special voting rights that could complicate the acquisition process or grant a third party unexpected influence after the deal closes.

Business Intelligence and Market Reputation

We gather information on the other party's market reputation and relationships with regulators and competitors, information that doesn't appear in official documents but could reveal important operational or reputational risks before making the final investment decision.

Environmental Review for Industrial Facilities

When the deal involves an industrial facility, we verify its environmental record and any pending violations that could transfer as liability to the new owner, a review essential especially for acquisitions of facilities in Jubail or other Eastern Province industrial zones.

Preparing the Final Due Diligence Report

We summarize the review findings in a clear report classifying risks by severity and manageability, and provide practical recommendations: proceed with the deal as is, negotiate a price or terms adjustment, or withdraw entirely if risks are material and unmanageable.

Financial Due Diligence Coordinated With Accountants

We work alongside independent accountants and financial auditors to examine the target party's financial statements, since legal review alone isn't enough without independent financial verification of the accuracy of declared figures and their freedom from hidden obligations or debts not properly recorded in the books.

Due Diligence in Cross-Border Acquisition Deals

When the target party is registered outside the Kingdom or has international operations, due diligence becomes more complex, requiring understanding of the multiple legal systems involved, and we coordinate with correspondent firms in those countries to ensure comprehensive coverage of every legal risk aspect across borders.

Post-Deal Follow-Up and Ongoing Compliance

Our responsibility doesn't end once the deal is signed; we help clients follow up on implementation of any representations or warranties the contract specified after completion, and confirm the other party is honoring their commitments during the post-deal transition period.

Due Diligence on Key Employees and Critical Talent

In many deals, the target company's real value lies in its management and technical team, not just its physical assets. We help investors assess the risk of key talent departure after the deal, and draft retention or incentive clauses ensuring this talent's continuity during the transition period.

Assessing Hidden Tax Risks Before Completing the Deal

We verify the target party's compliance with its tax and zakat obligations before the Zakat, Tax and Customs Authority, since any delayed or disputed tax claim could transfer as liability to the new owner after acquisition completes, a risk many hasty investors overlook.

Due Diligence in Energy and Heavy Industry Deals

Acquisition deals in the Eastern Province's energy and heavy industry sector carry additional complexity related to long-term environmental and industrial licenses, and we pay special attention to reviewing these licenses and deferred maintenance obligations that may not appear clearly in the target company's ordinary financial statements.

Frequently Asked Questions

How long does due diligence for a mid-size acquisition deal take?

It varies with the deal's complexity and number of parties involved, but usually takes two to four weeks, and we set a precise timeline after an initial review of the deal.

We discovered an unresolved regulatory violation at the target party. Does that stop the deal?

Not necessarily; we assess the violation's severity and resolvability, and help you negotiate a price adjustment or indemnity clause instead of full withdrawal.

Does due diligence include reviewing intellectual property too?

Yes, we verify trademark and patent registrations are valid and free of any ownership dispute that could affect their value.

The target party is refusing to disclose some financial documents. Is that a negative sign?

It could be; refusing full disclosure at the due diligence stage warrants additional investigation, and we help you assess how serious this refusal is.

Can a smaller-scale due diligence be conducted for a relatively small deal?

Yes, we tailor the review's scope to the deal's size and expected risks, so a full expensive review isn't needed for every deal.

What if we discover a material problem after the deal has already been signed?

We review the representations and warranties clauses in the deal contract to determine the possibility of claiming compensation from the other party for information not honestly disclosed.
