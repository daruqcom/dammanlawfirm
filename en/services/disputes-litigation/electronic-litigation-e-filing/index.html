Electronic Litigation (E-filing)

Electronic Litigation & e-Filing Lawyers in Dammam

Electronic litigation allows filing and fully tracking lawsuits through the Najiz platform, speeding up procedures and reducing the need for your repeated attendance compared to traditional paper transactions.

E-Filing Consultation on WhatsApp
Electronic litigation is the system through which the Saudi Ministry of Justice moved most litigation procedures to the digital Najiz platform, from filing the statement of claim to tracking hearings and enforcing judgments. This shift means most of your cases can be followed without needing repeated physical court attendance, but it also requires precise understanding of the platform's workings to avoid procedural errors that could delay your case. The next step is reaching out on WhatsApp.

How Did the Najiz Platform Change Litigation Procedures in the Kingdom?

Before digital transformation, most litigation procedures required repeated physical attendance to submit documents and follow up on a case. Today, the Najiz platform allows filing a lawsuit, submitting memoranda, tracking hearing dates, and even attending some hearings remotely, entirely online. This shift has saved significant time and cost, especially for businesses managing multiple cases in different cities.

Filing the Statement of Claim Electronically

We handle preparing and filing the statement of claim through the Najiz platform, ensuring all formal requirements are met that could lead to rejection or delay if overlooked. Drafting the electronic filing requires precision equal to a traditional paper filing, despite the different technical interface used to submit it.

Tracking Hearings and Remote Dates

We track your hearing dates and attend some remotely when the case's nature allows, keeping you continuously informed of the case status without needing your repeated personal attendance, especially if you run a business that doesn't allow you to be free for repeated court attendance.

Submitting Electronic Memoranda and Responses

We prepare and submit legal memoranda and responses to the other party's memoranda through the platform within defined statutory deadlines, deadlines that can be relatively short in the electronic system, requiring readiness and rapid response to avoid missing any procedural deadline.

Enforcing Judgments Through the Electronic Enforcement Court

After a judgment is issued, we follow up the enforcement request electronically before the Enforcement Court, including requests to freeze bank accounts and impose travel bans, procedures that have become much faster after digital transformation compared to the previous paper system.

Technical Challenges of Electronic Litigation

Despite the electronic system's advantages, technical challenges sometimes arise: problems uploading a document in a certain format, delayed case status updates on the platform, or difficulty understanding an ambiguously worded electronic notice. We help clients overcome these technical obstacles to ensure they don't affect their case's progress.

Cases Still Requiring Physical Attendance

Not all procedures have been fully digitized; some sensitive hearings or cases requiring direct examination still need physical court attendance. We clarify to clients from the outset which stages of their case will be fully electronic and which will need personal attendance, avoiding any timeline surprise.

Electronic Litigation for Multi-Branch Businesses

Businesses operating in more than one Eastern Province city particularly benefit from electronic litigation, as it allows them to manage multiple cases in different courts without needing their in-house legal team to travel constantly, and we help them organize these multiple cases through the platform efficiently.

Electronic Notices and the Importance of Regular Monitoring

Case notices arrive via text messages and alerts within the Najiz platform, and sometimes through registered email, and we advise clients to monitor these channels regularly, since some statutory deadlines begin from the date of the electronic notice, not the date the party actually views it, making delayed monitoring a real risk to your procedural rights.

Electronic Signature and Authenticating Judicial Documents

The platform relies on a certified electronic signature mechanism to ensure the validity of attributing submitted documents to their owners, and we help clients understand this signature's requirements, especially when the client is a business needing to authorize a specific person to sign on its behalf within required regulatory authorities.

Archiving Electronic Documents and Referring Back to Them Later

An additional advantage of electronic litigation is the ability to easily refer back to any document or memorandum submitted in the case through the platform's digital archive, without needing to search paper files that could be lost or damaged, and we help clients benefit from this feature when needing to review the case's complete history.

Remote Advocacy Through Video Conferencing Technology

Some court hearings are now held through video conferencing technology, especially for parties or witnesses residing outside the city where the competent court sits. We help clients prepare technically and legally for these remote hearings, ensuring the same level of readiness as a traditional in-person hearing.

Protecting Case Data Confidentiality on the Electronic Platform

We always advise clients to protect their Najiz platform login credentials, since any unauthorized access to a case account could expose sensitive information to viewing or even tampering. We help major businesses establish internal protocols for managing access authority to this platform among their legal department staff.

Handling Cases Involving a Saudi Party and a Foreign Party

When one party to a lawsuit resides outside the Kingdom, some electronic procedures need additional attestations or representation through an authorized local agent, and we help clients understand these additional requirements to avoid delaying the case due to procedural gaps related to representing a foreign party before the platform.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I follow my case entirely without physical court attendance?

In many cases yes, but some sensitive hearings or direct examinations may still require physical attendance, and we clarify that to you in advance.

I faced a technical problem uploading a document via Najiz. What do I do?

Contact us immediately; we help you resolve the technical issue or submit the document through an alternative method to avoid missing any statutory deadline.

What's the legal difference between filing a statement of claim electronically versus on paper?

No difference in legal force, but the electronic filing's formal requirements need precision equal to avoid application rejection.

Can a judgment be enforced entirely electronically without attending the Enforcement Court?

Yes for most procedures like account freeze and travel ban requests, which have become much faster after digital transformation.

We represent a company managing cases in several Eastern Province cities. Does electronic litigation make managing them easier?

Yes significantly, as all cases can be followed through one platform without your team constantly traveling, and we help you organize these multiple files efficiently.

I received an ambiguously worded electronic notice from the platform. How do I understand what's required of me?

Send it to us immediately; we clarify precisely what is required and help you respond within the defined statutory deadline.
