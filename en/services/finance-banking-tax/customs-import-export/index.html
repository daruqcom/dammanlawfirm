Customs & Import-Export

Customs & Import-Export Lawyers in Dammam

Resolving customs clearance and tariff classification disputes for importing industrial and commercial businesses, from classification objections to releasing held shipments.

Customs Consultation on WhatsApp
Customs law covers customs classification disputes for imported goods, calculating due customs duties, and shipment holds at ports for various reasons. Dammam and Jubail ports are among the Kingdom's most active in industrial imports, making understanding this legal framework essential for any business relying on regular imports of equipment and raw materials. The next step is reaching out on WhatsApp.

Objecting to Customs Classification Decisions

We review customs classification decisions for imported goods, and when we discover an error in this classification leading to duties calculated higher than actually due, we help you file a statutory objection backed by necessary technical documents.

Precisely Calculating Customs Duties

We help importers confirm customs duties are precisely calculated per the goods' correct classification, and determine any exemptions or reductions that might apply to their activity or the type of imported goods.

Procedures for Releasing Held Shipments

When a shipment is held at Dammam or Jubail port, we immediately review the hold's reason and initiate statutory release procedures as quickly as possible, minimizing the delay's impact on the business's operations.

Origin Disputes and Preferential Trade Agreements

We help importers claim customs exemptions or reductions due under preferential trade agreements, including proving the goods' origin with correctly required documents.

Compliance With Export Requirements

We help exporting companies understand statutory export requirements, including any restrictions or special licenses that might be imposed on certain goods or specific export destinations.

Customs Penalties and Reduction Requests

We help businesses assess the possibility of challenging imposed customs penalties, especially in cases of simple or unintentional procedural violations that might deserve mitigation.

Customs Clearance Agent Contracts

We review contracting agreements with customs clearance agents, clarifying each party's liability for any procedural or classification errors leading to subsequent violations or penalties.

Temporary Import and Customs Free Zones

We help industrial businesses understand temporary import regulations for equipment used in specific projects, and options for benefiting from customs free zones if available for their activity.

Customs Compliance for Heavy Industrial Imports

We help major industrial businesses understand customs compliance requirements specific to importing heavy equipment and specialized spare parts, including any customs exemptions that might apply to their sector.

Declared Customs Value Disputes

When customs disputes the declared value of imported goods and assesses a higher value, we help importers submit original purchase documents and invoices to prove the true value and object to any excess duty calculation.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I object to an incorrect customs classification?

The objection is filed with the relevant customs authority, supported by technical documents proving the correct classification, and we help you prepare this objection.

What should I do if customs holds my shipment?

We review the reason for the hold immediately and initiate statutory release procedures to minimize the impact of the delay on your business.

Can we benefit from customs exemptions under a preferential trade agreement?

We help you assess this entitlement and prove the goods' origin with correctly required documents.

We were imposed a customs penalty for a simple procedural violation. Can it be challenged?

Yes in certain cases, and we help you assess this challenge's chances and submit it in a supported manner.

Our customs clearance agent misclassified our goods causing a penalty. Who bears liability?

We review your contract with the agent to determine their liability for this error and help you claim appropriate compensation.

We're temporarily importing equipment for a fixed-term project. Can we benefit from the temporary import system?

We help you understand this system's requirements and how to benefit from it to avoid paying full customs duties on equipment that will later be returned.

Customs assessed our imported goods at a higher value than our actual purchase price. How do we object?

We help you submit original purchase invoices and supporting documents to prove the true value and challenge the excess calculation.
